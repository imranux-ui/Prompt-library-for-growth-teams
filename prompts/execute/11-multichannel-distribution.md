## Prompt 11 — Multi-Channel Distribution Repurposer
### Phase: EXECUTE
### Version: 1.0
#
### REQUIRES: CLIENT_CONTEXT + a published long-form article (URL or text)
### OUTPUT:   5 channel-specific content pieces derived from the article
# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

## Task

[EXECUTE] Repurpose the provided article into channel-specific content for distribution.

Read the full article and extract the core insight, best stat, and most actionable point. Then produce all five outputs below. Each must be written in the loaded CLIENT_VOICE and adapted to the norms of that channel — not just a copy-paste excerpt.

---

**Output 1 — LinkedIn post (organic)**
- 150–250 words
- Hook in first line (no "I'm excited to share..." openings)
- One core insight or contrarian take from the article
- 2–3 short paragraphs or line-spaced sentences (LinkedIn formatting)
- Soft CTA to read the full article (link in first comment — note this)
- No more than 3 hashtags, at the end

**Output 2 — X/Twitter thread (3–5 tweets)**
- Tweet 1: hook — the most surprising or counterintuitive point
- Tweets 2–4: supporting points or steps (one idea per tweet, ≤280 chars each)
- Tweet 5: CTA + link

**Output 3 — Newsletter snippet (100–150 words)**
- Written as one section of a weekly newsletter
- Opens with a "why this matters now" framing
- Summarizes the article's key takeaway in 2–3 sentences
- Ends with a "read the full piece" link prompt

**Output 4 — Social card copy (headline + subheadline)**
- Headline: ≤8 words, bold claim or stat from the article
- Subheadline: ≤15 words, context or benefit
- (Graphic design team uses this for the visual)

**Output 5 — Internal Slack/team summary (3 bullets)**
- For the GrowthX team or client's internal comms
- What the article covers, why it matters for the client, what result we're tracking
